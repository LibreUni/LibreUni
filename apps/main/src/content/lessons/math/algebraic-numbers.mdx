# Algebraic Numbers

Algebraic numbers are complex numbers that are roots of non-zero polynomials with rational coefficients.

## Definition and Minimal Polynomials

A number $\alpha \in \mathbb{C}$ is **algebraic** if there exists a polynomial $P(x) \in \mathbb{Q}[x]$ such that $P(\alpha) = 0$. The unique monic polynomial of lowest degree that $\alpha$ satisfies is called its **minimal polynomial**.

## Algebraic Integers

An **algebraic integer** is a root of a monic polynomial with integer coefficients. For example, $\sqrt{2}$ is an algebraic integer, but $1/2$ is not (it is an algebraic number, but its minimal polynomial is $2x - 1$, which is not monic).

## Transcendental Numbers

Numbers that are not algebraic are called **transcendental**. Proving a number is transcendental is usually much harder. Famous examples include $\pi$ and $e$.
