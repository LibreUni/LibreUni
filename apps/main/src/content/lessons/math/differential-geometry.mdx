Differential geometry uses the techniques of differential and integral calculus to study the geometry of curves and surfaces.

## Geometry of Curves

A **space curve** is defined by a vector function $\mathbf{r}(t)$. The key invariants are **curvature** ($\kappa$) and **torsion** ($\tau$).

## Gaussian Curvature

For surfaces, **Gaussian Curvature** ($K$) is the product of the two principal curvatures. It is an **intrinsic** property, meaning it can be determined by measuring distances along the surface without reference to the surrounding space.

## Gauss-Bonnet Theorem

The **Gauss-Bonnet Theorem** links the total curvature of a surface to its topology (Euler characteristic $\chi$):
$$\int_M K dA + \int_{\partial M} k_g ds = 2\pi \chi(M)$$
