# Field Theory and Extensions

Fields are algebraic structures where addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division (by non-zero elements) are well-defined.

## Characteristic of a Field

The **characteristic** of a field $F$, denoted $\text{char}(F)$, is the smallest $p > 0$ such that $p \cdot 1 = 0$. If no such $p$ exists, the characteristic is zero.

## Field Extensions

If $F \subseteq K$, we say $K$ is a **field extension** of $F$. $K$ is then a vector space over $F$. The dimension $[K:F]$ is the **degree** of the extension.

### Algebraic and Transcendental Elements

An element $\alpha \in K$ is **algebraic** over $F$ if it is a root of some non-zero polynomial $p(x) \in F[x]$. Otherwise, $\alpha$ is **transcendental**.

<CodeRunner

  code={`def evaluate_poly(coeffs, x, mod=None):
    # coeffs are [a0, a1, a2, ...] for a0 + a1*x + ...
    res = 0
    for i, c in enumerate(coeffs):
        term = c * (x**i)
        res += term
    return res if mod is None else res % mod

## Splitting Fields

A **splitting field** of a polynomial $p(x) \in F[x]$ is the smallest extension $K$ in which $p(x)$ factors into linear factors.

## Algebraic Closure

A field $F$ is **algebraically closed** if every non-constant polynomial in $F[x]$ has a root in $F$. The **Fundamental Theorem of Algebra** states that $\mathbb{C}$ is algebraically closed.
