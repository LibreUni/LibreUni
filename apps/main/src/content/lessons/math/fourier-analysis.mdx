# Fourier Analysis

Fourier Analysis allows us to decompose complex, periodic signals into a sum of simple sine and cosine waves.

## Fourier Series

Any periodic function $f(x)$ with period $2L$ can be represented as:
$$f(x) = \frac{a_0}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left( a_n \cos\frac{n\pi x}{L} + b_n \sin\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)$$

## The Fourier Transform

For non-periodic functions, the **Fourier Transform** $\hat{f}(\xi)$ maps a function from the time (or space) domain to the frequency domain:
$$\hat{f}(\xi) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x) e^{-2\pi i x \xi} dx$$

## Parseval's Theorem

**Parseval's Theorem** states that the total energy of a signal in the time domain is equal to the total energy in the frequency domain:
$$\int |f(x)|^2 dx = \int |\hat{f}(\xi)|^2 d\xi$$
