Homological algebra is the branch of mathematics that studies homology in a general algebraic setting. It is a powerful tool used in algebraic topology, algebraic geometry, and number theory.

## Chain Complexes

A **chain complex** $(C_\bullet, d_\bullet)$ is a sequence of abelian groups (or modules) and homomorphisms:
$$\dots \xrightarrow{d_{n+1}} C_n \xrightarrow{d_n} C_{n-1} \xrightarrow{d_{n-1}} \dots$$
such that the composition of any two consecutive maps is zero: $d_n \circ d_{n+1} = 0$.

This condition implies that the **image** of $d_{n+1}$ is contained in the **kernel** of $d_n$: $im(d_{n+1}) \subseteq ker(d_n)$.

## Homology Groups

The $n$-th **homology group** is the quotient group:
$$H_n(C) = \frac{ker(d_n)}{im(d_{n+1})}$$
Elements of $ker(d_n)$ are called **cycles**, and elements of $im(d_{n+1})$ are called **boundaries**. Homology measures the extent to which the complex is not "exact."

## Exact Sequences

A sequence is **exact** if the image of each map is exactly the kernel of the next. A **short exact sequence** is a sequence of the form:
$$0 \to A \xrightarrow{f} B \xrightarrow{g} C \to 0$$
which implies that $f$ is injective, $g$ is surjective, and $im(f) = ker(g)$.

## Diagram Chasing and the Snake Lemma

Diagram chasing is a method of proof used in homological algebra to establish properties of morphisms by tracing elements through commutative diagrams. The **Snake Lemma** is a fundamental result that relates the kernels and cokernels of two morphisms between short exact sequences.
