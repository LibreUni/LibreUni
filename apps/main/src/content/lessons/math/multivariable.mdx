Multivariable calculus extends the concepts of single-variable calculus to functions of several variables.

## Partial Derivatives and the Gradient

A **partial derivative** measures the rate of change of a multivariable function with respect to one variable, holding others constant. The **Gradient** $\nabla f$ is a vector of partial derivatives:
$$\nabla f = \left( \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_1}, \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_2}, \dots, \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_n} \right)$$
It points in the direction of the steepest ascent.

## Multiple Integrals

A double integral $\iint_D f(x, y) \, dA$ calculates the volume under the surface $z = f(x, y)$ over a region $D$ in the $xy$-plane.

<CodeRunner client:load

print(f"Double integral of x*y over [0,1]x[0,2]: {area:.4f}")`}

## Vector Fields and Line Integrals

A vector field $\mathbf{F}$ assigns a vector to every point in space. A **line integral** $\int_C \mathbf{F} \cdot d\mathbf{r}$ measures the work done by a force field along a path $C$.

## The Jacobian Matrix

The Jacobian matrix of a vector-valued function $\mathbf{f}: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}^m$ is the matrix of all first-order partial derivatives. It represents the best linear approximation of the function near a point.
$$J_{ij} = \frac{\partial f_i}{\partial x_j}$$
