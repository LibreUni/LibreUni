The study of mathematics begins with numbers. We build complex systems from simple foundations, starting with the natural numbers.

## The Hierarchy of Numbers

1. **Natural Numbers** ($\mathbb{N}$): $\{0, 1, 2, 3, \dots\}$ (counting numbers).
2. **Integers** ($\mathbb{Z}$): $\{\dots, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, \dots\}$.
3. **Rational Numbers** ($\mathbb{Q}$): Fractions $p/q$ where $p, q \in \mathbb{Z}$ and $q \neq 0$.
4. **Real Numbers** ($\mathbb{R}$): Includes irrational numbers like $\sqrt{2}$ and $\pi$.
5. **Complex Numbers** ($\mathbb{C}$): Numbers of the form $a + bi$.

## Peano Axioms

The natural numbers are rigorously defined by the Peano axioms, which use a single constant $0$ and a successor function $S(n)$.
- $0$ is a natural number.
- For every $n$, $S(n)$ is a natural number.
- No two numbers have the same successor.
- $0$ is not the successor of any number.

## Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic

Every integer greater than 1 either is a prime number itself or can be represented as a unique product of prime numbers.

            i += 1
        else:
            n //= i
            factors.append(i)
    if n > 1:
        factors.append(n)
    return factors
