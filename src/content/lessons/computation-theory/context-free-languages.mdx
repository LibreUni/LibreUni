# Context-Free Languages and Grammars

Context-free grammars describe recursively nested structure. They are the mathematical basis for many parsers because one nonterminal can expand independently of surrounding context. This independence is powerful enough to express balanced parentheses and matching counts, but limited enough that some cross-serial or copying patterns remain impossible. The central skill is to move among grammar rules, derivations, parse trees, pushdown machines, and proofs about the generated language.

## Formal grammar semantics

A context-free grammar is a quadruple (G=(V,Sigma,R,S)), where V is a finite set of variables (nonterminals), (Sigma) is a finite terminal alphabet disjoint from V, R is a finite set of productions (Atoalpha) with Ain V and (alphain(VcupSigma)^*), and S is the start variable. The language (L(G)) contains terminal strings derivable from S.

A derivation replaces one variable at a time. In a leftmost derivation, always replace the leftmost remaining variable; in a rightmost derivation, always replace the rightmost. A parse tree records the hierarchical choices independently of derivation order. If a tree’s leaves from left to right spell w, then S derives w. Conversely, every derivation has a corresponding parse tree.

**Worked grammar.** Let (Sto 0S1midepsilon). Repeating the recursive rule n times and then using (epsilon) derives (0^n1^n). The tree has a nested chain: each 0 is introduced before the recursive child and the matching 1 after it. This is a stack-like dependency; a finite automaton cannot retain arbitrary n, but a pushdown automaton can push each 0 and pop for each 1.

To prove (L(G)=L), prove soundness (SRightarrow^*wRightarrow win L) and completeness (win LRightarrow SRightarrow^*w). For recursive grammars, induction on derivation height or string structure is usually appropriate. A grammar that generates all desired strings but also one unwanted string is not correct; both inclusions matter.

## Ambiguity

A grammar is ambiguous if some string has two distinct parse trees, equivalently two distinct leftmost derivations. The grammar (Eto E+Emid E*Emid id) is ambiguous: the string "id+id*id" can parse with addition at the root or multiplication at the root. Ambiguity matters because parse structure often determines program meaning.

Precedence and associativity can be encoded structurally. For multiplication binding more tightly than addition, use (Eto E+Tmid T), (Tto T*Fmid F), (Fto(E)mid id). Left recursion represents left associativity, while separate levels represent precedence. Removing ambiguity is a design and proof task, not a cosmetic rewrite.

Some context-free languages are inherently ambiguous: every grammar generating them is ambiguous. In such cases, no unambiguous context-free grammar can serve as the complete representation, although a parser may choose one interpretation or impose external disambiguation. Do not confuse a particular ambiguous grammar with inherent ambiguity.

## Chomsky normal form

For many theorems and algorithms, convert a grammar to Chomsky normal form (CNF), where productions have (Ato BC) or (Ato a), with a possible (Stoepsilon) exception. Eliminate useless symbols, epsilon-productions, and unit productions, then binarize long right sides and introduce variables for terminals in mixed rules. Preserve the language, except for the carefully handled empty string.

The conversion is constructive but can increase grammar size. Each transformation requires a language-equivalence argument. Removing (Atoepsilon) must add alternatives where occurrences of A are omitted; unit elimination must add the terminal behavior reachable through unit chains; binarization merely names subexpressions. A proof that lists transformations without checking the start-symbol exception is incomplete.

## The CYK viewpoint

Given a CNF grammar and input w of length n, the CYK algorithm fills a table (T[i,ell]) of variables that derive the substring of length ell beginning at i. Base cells contain variables producing the terminal. For each split k, add A when a rule (Ato BC) has B in the left cell and C in the right cell. The start variable in (T[0,n]) means acceptance.

The recurrence is justified by parse-tree roots. A CNF tree for a substring of length greater than one has a root A with two children B and C, and the yield splits at some k. Conversely, combining two table witnesses gives a parse tree. There are O(n^2) cells and, with a fixed grammar, O(n) splits per cell, so the basic running time is O(n^3) and space O(n^2). The algorithm decides membership, not necessarily a unique parse; storing backpointers can recover one or all parses with additional output cost.

## Pushdown automata connection

A pushdown automaton (PDA) is a finite control machine with a stack. It can push symbols while reading an opening structure and pop them while reading a matching closing structure. A nondeterministic PDA recognizes a language when at least one legal computation accepts. Nondeterministic PDAs recognize exactly the context-free languages. The stack is unbounded but access is restricted to its top, which explains both the power and the limitation.

For the grammar (Sto 0S1midepsilon), the PDA pushes a marker for each 0, nondeterministically chooses the midpoint, then pops one marker per 1, accepting when the input and required stack markers are exhausted. The conversion between grammar and PDA is a formal equivalence theorem. It does not mean every parser must literally use a nondeterministic stack; deterministic subsets and parsing algorithms make additional choices.

## Closure and non-closure

Context-free languages are closed under union, concatenation, and Kleene star. They are closed under intersection with regular languages and under homomorphism, but not under arbitrary intersection or complement. Non-closure is a warning against importing regular-language intuition without proof.

To prove that a language is not context-free, the pumping lemma for CFLs decomposes a sufficiently long string as (uvxyz), with |vxy| bounded, |vy| greater than 0, and (uv^ixy^iz) remaining in the language for all i. The two pumped regions may lie in different structural blocks, so case analysis is necessary. Ogden’s lemma strengthens control by marking positions, often producing cleaner proofs. As with the regular pumping lemma, the quantifiers require defeating every permitted decomposition.

**Worked non-context-free strategy.** For (L=()0^n1^n2^n:nge0()), choose (0^p1^p2^p). A bounded window vxy can cover at most two of the three blocks. Pumping changes at least one block without changing all three equally, yielding a string outside L. The proof must enumerate whether the window lies within one block or crosses one boundary; it cannot cross both when its length is bounded by p.

## Exercises

1. Prove by induction that (Sto 0S1midepsilon) generates exactly (()0^n1^n:nge0()).
2. Give two parse trees for the string "id+id*id" under (Eto E+Emid E*Emid id).
3. Rewrite an arithmetic grammar to encode precedence and left associativity.
4. Convert a small grammar to CNF and justify each transformation.
5. Fill a CYK table for a short string and explain one backpointer.
6. Give a full pumping-lemma-for-CFLs proof that (()0^n1^n2^n:nge0()) is not context-free.
7. Explain why closure under intersection with regular languages is useful for isolating structure.

### Diagnostic solutions


1. Every recursive expansion introduces one 0 before and one 1 after the recursive yield, so induction on derivation depth gives (0^n1^n). Conversely, for any n, apply the recursive rule n times and epsilon once, producing the target string.
2. One tree has root + with left id and right subtree (id*id); the other has root * with left subtree (id+id) and right id. Distinct roots demonstrate ambiguity and different conventional meanings.
3. Use E→E+T|T, T→T*F|F, F→(E)|id. E’s recursive rule groups additions, T groups multiplications, and left recursion yields left associativity. Prove by induction on parse-tree shape that multiplication occurs below addition.
4. Remove epsilon and unit rules while adding omitted-variable alternatives; remove unreachable or non-generating variables; replace terminals in mixed right sides with variables; and binarize long right sides. Check the empty string separately through a fresh start symbol.
5. Base cells contain variables producing each terminal. For each longer interval, inspect all binary splits and add A when its two child variables match a rule. A backpointer records the split and child variables, so recursively following it reconstructs a parse tree.
6. Assume a pumping length p and choose (0^p1^p2^p). For any (uvxyz) with |vxy|le p and |vy| greater than 0, v and y affect at most two adjacent blocks. Pump i=0 or 2; the counts of all three symbols cannot remain equal, so the pumped string is outside the language, contradiction.
7. Intersecting with a regular filter can force a candidate language into a canonical block order without leaving the context-free class. If the result is known non-context-free, closure yields a contradiction. The filter must be regular and the exact intersection must be proved.



## Synthesis checkpoint

For every context-free construction, record whether the grammar is intended to generate exactly the language, whether ambiguity is allowed, and which normal form or parser invariant is being used. A derivation is evidence of membership; a failed derivation is not automatically evidence of nonmembership without a parser or theorem. Keep the language definition, grammar, and proof obligation aligned.
