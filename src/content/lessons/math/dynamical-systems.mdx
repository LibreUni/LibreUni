# Dynamical Systems

A **Dynamical System** is a triplet $(T, M, \Phi)$ where $T$ is the time domain, $M$ is the **Phase Space** (manifold of possible states), and $\Phi: T \times M \to M$ is an evolution function satisfying:
1. $\Phi(0, x) = x$ (Identity)
2. $\Phi(t, \Phi(s, x)) = \Phi(t+s, x)$ (Group property)

## Discrete Dynamics: Iterated Maps

In a discrete system, the state is updated by a map $f: M \to M$:
$$x_{n+1} = f(x_n)$$

The sequence $\{x_n\}_{n=0}^\infty$ is called the **Orbit** of $x_0$.

## Fixed Points and Stability

A **fixed point** $x^*$ satisfies $f(x^*) = x^*$. 
- A point is **Lyapunov Stable** if for every $\epsilon > 0$, there exists $\delta > 0$ such that $d(x_0, x^*) < \delta \implies d(x_n, x^*) < \epsilon$ for all $n > 0$.
- For a differentiable map $f: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$, $x^*$ is **Asymptotically Stable** if $|f'(x^*)| < 1$.

## Continuous Dynamics: Flow

Continuous systems are defined by a vector field $\mathbf{V}: M \to TM$ and an autonomous differential equation:
$$\dot{\mathbf{x}} = \frac{d\mathbf{x}}{dt} = \mathbf{f}(\mathbf{x})$$

The solution through $x_0$ is the **Integral Curve** or **Flow Line**. Stability is determined by the eigenvalues of the **Jacobian Matrix** $J = \nabla \mathbf{f}$. If all eigenvalues have negative real parts, the equilibrium is stable.
