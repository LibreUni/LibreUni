# Solvability by Radicals and the Abel-Ruffini Theorem

Why does a quadratic formula exist, yet no universal formula using radicals exists for degree 5 or higher? The answer lies in the structure of Galois groups.

## The Quest for the Quintic

- **1515:** Scipione del Ferro solves depressed cubics ($x^3 + cx = d$).
- **1535:** Niccolò Tartaglia independently rediscovers the cubic solution.
- **1545:** Gerolamo Cardano publishes *Ars Magna* with the cubic and Lodovico Ferrari's quartic solution.
- **1824:** Niels Henrik Abel proves that no general radical formula exists for degree 5.
- **1832:** Évariste Galois (writing the night before his death in a duel) fully characterizes solvability by radicals in terms of group theory.

---

## The Galois Criterion for Solvability

> **Theorem (Galois):** A polynomial $f(x) \in K[x]$ is solvable by radicals if and only if its Galois group $\text{Gal}(L/K)$ (where $L$ is the splitting field) is a **solvable group**.

Recall: A group $G$ is **solvable** if it has a chain
$$\{e\} = G_0 \trianglelefteq G_1 \trianglelefteq \dots \trianglelefteq G_k = G$$
where each factor $G_{i+1}/G_i$ is Abelian.

---

## The Insolvability of the Quintic

For a general polynomial of degree $n$, the Galois group of its splitting field is $S_n$.

| $n$ | $S_n$ solvable? | Radical solution? |
|-----|----------------|-------------------|
| 1, 2 | Yes | Trivial, $x = -b/a$ |
| 3 | Yes | Cubic formula (Cardano) |
| 4 | Yes | Quartic formula (Ferrari) |
| $\ge 5$ | **No** | **No universal formula** |

$S_5$ has a composition series $\{e\} \trianglelefteq A_5 \trianglelefteq S_5$ where $S_5/A_5 \cong C_2$ (Abelian) but $A_5$ is **simple and non-Abelian** — it has no non-trivial normal subgroups, so it cannot be broken down into Abelian steps.

## Worked Example: $S_4$ is Solvable

$S_4$ has the composition series:
$$\{e\} \trianglelefteq V_4 \trianglelefteq A_4 \trianglelefteq S_4$$
where $V_4$ (Klein four-group) is Abelian, $A_4/V_4 \cong C_3$, and $S_4/A_4 \cong C_2$. All factors are Abelian.

## Edge Cases

- **Solvable:** $S_3$: $\{e\} \trianglelefteq A_3 \trianglelefteq S_3$ (Abelian factors).
- **Simple non-Abelian:** $A_5$ (order 60) is the smallest.
- **Non-Galois:** $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt[3]{2})/\mathbb{Q}$ has trivial Galois group — not normal.

### Challenge Problem

Prove that $A_5$ is simple.
