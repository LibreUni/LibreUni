# Galois Theory

Galois theory connects field theory and group theory, providing a way to study field extensions by examining their automorphism groups.

## Galois Groups

The **Galois group** of a field extension $K/F$, denoted $\text{Gal}(K/F)$, is the group of automorphisms of $K$ that fix $F$ point-wise.
$$\sigma \in \text{Gal}(K/F) \iff \sigma(a) = a \text{ for all } a \in F$$

## Fundamental Theorem of Galois Theory

For a finite Galois extension $K/F$, there is a one-to-one correspondence between:
1. Subfields $E$ such that $F \subseteq E \subseteq K$.
2. Subgroups $H$ of $\text{Gal}(K/F)$.

The mapping is defined by $E \mapsto \text{Gal}(K/E)$ and $H \mapsto K^H$ (the fixed field of $H$).

## Solvability by Radicals

A polynomial equation is **solvable by radicals** if and only if its Galois group is a **solvable group**. 
This explains why there is no general formula for the roots of a quintic (fifth-degree) polynomial, as the symmetric group $S_5$ is not solvable.
